Delivery Rationale

Justification for the hybrid delivery model and programme structure for EQA and management reference.

Hybrid Delivery Model

The Focus Awards Level 3 Certificate in Assessing Vocational Achievement (RQF) is delivered by PT Academy using a hybrid model combining pre-recorded instructional content with live online group sessions. This approach is fully consistent with the RQF delivery requirements for this qualification. Pre-recorded content counts toward guided learning hours where it is curriculum-led, structured to the unit learning outcomes, and accessible via a managed learning environment. All pre-recorded sessions are mapped to specific units and criteria.

Live Group Sessions

Six live group sessions of two hours each are scheduled across the programme. These sessions are tutor-led and serve as consolidation, calibration, evidence review, and action planning points rather than primary knowledge delivery. This reflects current best practice in vocational assessor training, where the majority of competence is demonstrated through practice and evidence gathering rather than classroom instruction.

Programme Duration

The programme operates on a flexible 6 to 8 week timeline. The core taught and evidenced content is designed around six weeks. Additional weeks may be used where learners require additional time to complete evidence gathering cycles, obtain signatures, or finalise their portfolio. The flexible end point ensures all learners can meet the full assessment criteria without the programme being compressed unreasonably.

Programme Phases

Phase 1

Orientation

Weeks 1 and 2. Programme induction, Unit 1 knowledge, learner allocation and Assessment Plan 1.

Phase 2

Consolidation

Weeks 3 to 5. Evidence gathering cycles, observation records, Unit 2 and 3 completion, reflective commentary.

Phase 3

Review and Sign-Off

Week 6 onwards. Portfolio audit, IQA preparation, submission and programme close.
