Introduction

What Is This Qualification?

The Level 3 Certificate in Assessing Vocational Achievement (CAVA) is a nationally recognised qualification awarded by Focus Awards. It is designed for anyone working in a training or coaching environment who assesses the competence, skills, knowledge, and understanding of learners.

As a PT Academy learner, you will complete written questions, observation records, and a portfolio of evidence to demonstrate your ability to assess others.

Qualification Structure

The Level 3 CAVA is made up of three units. You are required to complete all three. Unit 1 covers the underpinning theory and principles of assessment.

Unit 1: Understanding the Principles and Practices of Assessment

The theory unit. Covers the functions of assessment, types of assessment, quality assurance, and the legal and ethical responsibilities of an assessor. Assessed through written questions.

Unit 2: Assess Occupational Competence in the Work Environment

The practical unit. You will plan and carry out work-based assessments with your PT learners, using direct observation, work products, and professional discussion to judge competence.

Unit 3: Assess Vocational Skills, Knowledge and Understanding

The knowledge assessment unit. You will assess learners in classroom or theory-based settings using a range of methods including assignments, oral questioning, and skills tests.

Your Two Learners

Throughout this qualification you must work with two PT learners at different levels:

  • Learner 1: Level 2 Gym Instructing
  • Learner 2: Level 3 Personal Training

You must use a minimum of 7 different assessment methods across both learners. Evidence from Units 2 and 3 is linked and the Observation Record maps across both units.

Requirements
Unit 1: Understanding the Principles and Practices of Assessment
  • Answer all written questions fully and in your own words
  • Learning Outcome 1: Functions of assessment
  • Learning Outcome 2: Legislation, equality, and ethics
  • Learning Outcome 3: Peer and professional relationships
  • Learning Outcome 4: Quality assurance
Unit 2: Assess Occupational Competence in the Work Environment
  • Learning Outcome 1: Evidence Gathering and Reflection
  • Learning Outcome 2: Evidence Gathering and Reflection
  • Learning Outcome 3: Evidence Gathering and Reflection
  • Learning Outcome 4: Evidence Gathering and Reflection
  • Observation Record (shared with Unit 3 — maps across both units)
Unit 3: Assess Vocational Skills, Knowledge and Understanding
  • Learning Outcome 1: Evidence Gathering and Reflection
  • Learning Outcome 2: Evidence Gathering and Reflection
  • Learning Outcome 3: Evidence Gathering and Reflection
  • Learning Outcome 4: Evidence Gathering and Reflection
  • Observation Record (same record as Unit 2 — criteria map into each other)

The 7 Mandatory Assessment Methods

Across both learners and both units, you must demonstrate the use of all seven methods:

  • Recognition of Prior Learning (RPL)
  • Direct Observation
  • Examining Work Products
  • Questioning (oral or written)
  • Witness Testimony and Expert Witness
  • Learner Statement and Professional Discussion
  • Simulation (where appropriate)
