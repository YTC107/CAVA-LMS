What Is This Qualification?

The Level 3 Certificate in Assessing Vocational Achievement (CAVA) is a nationally recognised qualification awarded by Focus Awards. It is designed for anyone working in a training or coaching environment who assesses the competence, skills, knowledge, and understanding of learners.

As a PT Academy learner, you will complete written questions, observation records, and a portfolio of evidence to demonstrate your ability to assess others.

2

Complete your Assessment Plan

Open the Assessment Plan in the tab and fill in your planned completion dates for each unit. Your assessor will countersign this with you.

Unit 1 is knowledge-based. You must answer all written questions fully and in your own words. These cover the principles, types, and legal responsibilities of assessment.

4

Qualification Structure

The Level 3 CAVA is made up of three units. You are required to complete all three. Click each unit below to read more.

The theory unit. Covers the functions of assessment, types of assessment, quality assurance, and the legal and ethical responsibilities of an assessor. Assessed through written questions across 8 learning outcomes.

Unit reference: A/601/5313

The practical unit. You will plan and carry out work-based assessments with your PT learners using direct observation, work products, and professional discussion to judge competence.

Unit reference: H/601/5314

  • Observation of performance in the work environment
  • Examining products of work
  • Questioning the learner
  • Discussing with the learner
  • Use of others (witness testimony)
  • Looking at learner statements
  • Recognising prior learning

The knowledge assessment unit. You will assess learners in classroom or theory-based settings using a range of methods.

Unit reference: F/601/5319

  • Assessments in simulated environments
  • Skills tests
  • Oral and written questions
  • Assignments
  • Projects
  • Case studies
  • Recognising prior learning

Your Two Learners

Throughout this qualification you must work with two PT learners at different levels:

  • Learner 1: Level 2 Gym Instructing
  • Learner 2: Level 3 Personal Training

You must use a minimum of 7 different assessment methods across both learners. Evidence from Units 2 and 3 is linked and the Observation Record maps across both units.

Requirements

Below are the specific requirements for each unit. Click a unit to expand the details.

All 25 questions must be answered in full. Answers should be in your own words and reflect your own practice. Evidence must be authentic, sufficient, and current.

  • Learning Outcome 1: Understand the principles and requirements of assessment
  • Learning Outcome 2: Understand different types of assessment methods
  • Learning Outcome 3: Understand how to plan assessment
  • Learning Outcome 4: Understand how to involve learners and others in assessment
  • Learning Outcome 5: Understand how to make assessment decisions
  • Learning Outcome 6: Understand quality assurance of the assessment process
  • Learning Outcome 7: Understand how to manage information relating to assessment
  • Learning Outcome 8: Understand the legal and good practice requirements in relation to assessment
  • Learning Outcome 1: Be able to plan the assessment of occupational competence
  • Learning Outcome 2: Be able to make assessment decisions about occupational competence
  • Learning Outcome 3: Be able to give feedback and support learners
  • Learning Outcome 4: Be able to contribute to the internal quality assurance process
  • Observation Record required (shared with Unit 3)
  • Learning Outcome 1: Be able to prepare assessments of vocational skills, knowledge and understanding
  • Learning Outcome 2: Be able to carry out assessments of vocational skills, knowledge and understanding
  • Learning Outcome 3: Be able to give feedback and support learners
  • Learning Outcome 4: Be able to contribute to the internal quality assurance process
  • Observation Record required (same record as Unit 2)

The 7 Mandatory Assessment Methods

Across both learners and both units, you must demonstrate the use of all seven methods:

  • Recognition of Prior Learning (RPL)
  • Direct Observation
  • Examining Work Products
  • Questioning (oral or written)
  • Witness Testimony and Expert Witness
  • Learner Statement and Professional Discussion
  • Simulation (where appropriate)
