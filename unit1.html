Unit Overview

This unit develops your knowledge and understanding of the fundamental principles and practices that underpin assessment. You will explore how assessment is designed, delivered, and evaluated to ensure it meets the needs of learners and organisations. All learning outcomes in this unit must be assessed using methods appropriate to the assessment of knowledge and understanding.

Before You Begin: Understanding Command Verbs

DEFINE
State the meaning of a term, concept, or item. Your answer should be concise, accurate and informative.
Example: Define the term ‘validity’ in assessment
IDENTIFY
Pick out something; recognise; find; point out. Use a present-state to distinguish from among a number of possibilities.
Example: Identify three different assessment methods
DESCRIBE
Give a detailed account of something, including relevant features, characteristics, and context.
Example: Describe the role of different testing methods
EXPLAIN
Make something clear by describing it in detail, as it is; explain in detail; indicate how or why.
Example: Explain why different assessment methods have varying levels of validity
COMPARE
Set out the points of similarity and difference between two things. Pairs of items must be compared fairly.
Example: Compare formative and summative assessment methods
SUMMARISE
Give a brief condensed account of the main points; include only the key features or aspects.
Example: Summarise the key principles of effective assessment
